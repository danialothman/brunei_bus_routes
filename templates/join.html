Brunei Bus Routes · Field data collection

Help map Brunei's buses — and get paid per route

We're building a free, open, machine-readable map of every public bus route in Brunei — the kind of data that powers journey planners and Google Maps. The catch: the only nationwide dataset we have is from 2016 and it's full of gaps. We need people on the ground to ride the routes and capture what's really there. You pick the routes, do the legwork, and earn a bounty for each route you complete.

Why this matters

Open the trip planner and you'll see a warning on the 2016 data: routes are derived from incomplete KML files, so many are missing stops, and journeys come out with detours that don't exist in real life. The only way to fix that is to go and observe the buses — where they actually run, where they actually stop, and when. That's the job.

The work — one route at a time

For each route you take on, you capture four things:

🛰️

Ride & record

Ride the route end to end with a GPS track running (any phone tracking app), so we get the real road path.

🚏

Log the stops

Note every stop in order — its name, any stop code on the sign, and where it is. Order matters; it's the order passengers experience.

📷

Photograph signboards

Photograph each stop's signboard and the route's timing board, so names and departure times can be transcribed accurately.

✅

Submit for review

Send it in through the tools we provide. We run a quick quality check, then it's accepted and you're paid.

Per-route bounty

Rates above are indicative — final amounts are confirmed on onboarding. Quality and accuracy beat speed: a route that needs redoing isn't accepted.

What you'll need

  • You're based in Brunei and can travel the routes.
  • A smartphone with working GPS and a camera.
  • Reliability and attention to detail — the data has to be trustworthy.
  • Your own transport helps for far or rural routes, but isn't required.
  • No technical background needed — we provide the tools and a guide.
